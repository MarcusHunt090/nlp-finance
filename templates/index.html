Methodology

A detailed walkthrough of the NLP pipeline, feature engineering, model design, and system architecture.

1

Data Collection

Financial headlines labeled positive, negative, or neutral. 9,772 training and 2,443 test samples — merged from Financial PhraseBank and Twitter Financial News Sentiment datasets.

2

Text Preprocessing

Tokenization via NLTK. Stopword removal with preserved negation words. Negation handling: tokens after "not", "no", "never" receive a NOT_ prefix until a clause boundary, flipping their semantic polarity.

3

Feature Extraction

TF-IDF with sublinear TF, bigrams (1,2-grams), min_df=2, max_df=0.95, 5,000 features. Plus a Bag-of-Words CountVectorizer baseline for Naive Bayes comparison.

4

Classical Models

Three classifiers trained independently:

  • Logistic Regression — interpretable coefficients, TF-IDF
  • Naive Bayes — probabilistic baseline, Bag-of-Words
  • Random Forest — 100 trees, captures non-linear patterns
5

FinBERT Transformer

ProsusAI/finbert — BERT pre-trained on financial corpora — loads asynchronously and becomes the 4th model with 40% ensemble weight when ready. Understands domain context that bag-of-words models miss.

6

Ensemble Prediction

Weighted probability averaging: LR 35%, RF 30%, NB 20%. With FinBERT active: FinBERT 40%, others rescaled to 60%. Reduces variance and improves generalization over any single model.

7

Entity Detection & Stock Correlation

Regex + dictionary matching identifies 30+ ticker symbols and company names. Detected tickers fetch real-time 30-day price history via yfinance, enabling visual correlation between news sentiment and stock performance.

8

Persistence & Feedback Loop

All predictions and user corrections stored in SQLite. Corrections feed back into model retraining on demand. History enables sentiment trend analysis over time.
